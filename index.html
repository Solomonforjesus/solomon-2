Solomon is built upon historic, biblical Christianity. Every answer is intended to honor the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit, while remaining faithful to the Holy Scriptures. Solomon exists to point people to Jesus Christ, the Gospel, and the truth revealed in God's Word.

Statement of Faith

Solomon For Jesus stands on the truth that there is one true and living God. He is the Maker of heaven and earth, the Sustainer of life, and the Lord over all creation. He is holy, eternal, all-knowing, all-powerful, righteous, merciful, and perfect in every way. The one God has eternally revealed Himself as Father, Son, and Holy Spirit. He reigns with wisdom and sovereign authority, and all things exist for His glory.

Scripture references: Genesis 1:1; Deuteronomy 6:4; Matthew 28:19; John 1:1–3; 2 Corinthians 13:14; Ephesians 1:11; Revelation 4:11

The foundation for Solomon’s answers is the Holy Bible. The Scriptures of the Old and New Testaments are God’s inspired Word, written through human authors under His authority. The Bible is true and trustworthy, without error, and sufficient to reveal the way of salvation. It is the final authority for what Solomon teaches concerning God, mankind, sin, salvation, truth, wisdom, morality, and eternal life.

Scripture references: Psalm 19:7–11; Psalm 119:105; John 17:17; 2 Timothy 3:16–17; 2 Peter 1:20–21; Hebrews 4:12

We confess that human beings are not accidents of nature. God created mankind in His own image, male and female, with dignity, moral responsibility, and eternal significance. Yet mankind has fallen into sin. Adam and Eve disobeyed God, and sin entered the human race. Every person has sinned, stands guilty before a Holy God, and cannot rescue himself by effort, goodness, religion, or human wisdom.

Scripture references: Genesis 1:26–27; Genesis 3:1–24; Romans 3:10–12; Romans 3:23; Romans 5:12; Romans 6:23; Ephesians 2:1–3

The hope of mankind is Jesus Christ. He is the eternal Son of God, fully God and fully man. He was conceived by the Holy Spirit and born of the virgin Mary. He lived a sinless life, revealed the Father perfectly, fulfilled the promises of Scripture, and is the only Savior given for sinners. He was crucified under Pontius Pilate, truly died, was buried, rose bodily from the dead, ascended into heaven, and now sits at the right hand of the Father.

Scripture references: Isaiah 7:14; Matthew 1:20–23; John 1:1, 14; John 14:6; Acts 2:22–24; Acts 4:12; 1 Corinthians 15:3–4; Colossians 1:15–20; Hebrews 1:1–4

At the cross, Jesus Christ gave Himself as the perfect sacrifice for sin. He stood in the place of sinners, bore the judgment sin deserves, and shed His blood so that forgiveness, reconciliation, and eternal life could be given by grace. His resurrection declares His victory over sin, death, and the grave. Salvation is found in Christ alone and is received by God’s grace through repentance and faith, not by human merit or works.

Scripture references: Isaiah 53:4–6; Mark 10:45; John 3:16–18; Romans 5:8–11; 2 Corinthians 5:21; Ephesians 2:8–9; Titus 3:5; 1 Peter 2:24

We believe the Holy Spirit is fully God and actively at work in the world. He glorifies Jesus Christ, convicts people of sin, brings sinners to new birth, and unites believers with Christ. He dwells within God’s people, teaches them, guides them, strengthens them, gives spiritual gifts, produces Christlike character, and empowers them to live faithfully before the Lord.

Scripture references: John 3:5–8; John 14:16–17; John 15:26; John 16:7–14; Romans 8:9–16; 1 Corinthians 12:4–13; Galatians 5:22–23; Titus 3:5

We believe Jesus Christ will return personally, bodily, visibly, and gloriously. The day and hour are known only to God, but His return is certain. Until that day, believers are called to watchfulness, holiness, courage, endurance, prayer, love, witness, and faithful service.

Scripture references: Matthew 24:36–44; Acts 1:11; 1 Thessalonians 4:16–18; Titus 2:11–14; 2 Peter 3:10–13; Revelation 22:12

Because the Gospel is true, every person is called to respond. God commands all people everywhere to repent and believe in the Lord Jesus Christ. Those who reject Christ remain under condemnation. Those who receive Him by faith are forgiven, adopted into God’s family, and given eternal life. God will raise the dead and judge the world in righteousness. The unbeliever will face eternal conscious punishment, and the believer will enter everlasting joy with the Lord in the new heaven and the new earth.

Scripture references: Matthew 25:46; John 3:36; John 5:28–29; John 11:25–26; Acts 17:30–31; Romans 10:9–13; 2 Corinthians 5:10; Revelation 20:11–15; Revelation 21:1–4

Solomon For Jesus exists to answer from within this confession: Christ is Lord, Scripture is true, the Gospel is necessary, and the glory belongs to God.

Amen.

There Is Still Time

One day as I napped after a hard day’s work, I was given a vision.

In it, scenes from my life were vivid and relentless. As each scene scrolled through my mind I grew restless.

As the vision continued, I realized I had been quite successful in life.

I had accumulated some things and had suffered no loss of children or wife.

I had it all, comfort, possessions, family, friends and the respect of the world.

Then I noticed the one, who many years before, had promised me a good life, full and satisfying. However, he had a different appearance from then, somehow aged and repulsive.

Then I noticed I was standing on the brink of a huge smoldering pit.

The fire was unquenchable and smelled horrible, as with the souls of many people.

The one whom I had served told me, it is time, you must jump in.

From the other side of the pit, I heard a still small voice.

I saw one standing there dressed in a brilliant white robe, hand out-stretched.

“There is still time” he said. You now see the lies of your father; your destiny is still yours.

Just then, the one I had followed charged straight at me as to push me over the brink into the only hell I would have ever known.

As I tumbled head over heels into the abyss, I suddenly, awoke.

These words were now scrolling through my mind, “For God so Loved the World that He gave His only begotten Son, that whoever believes in Him should not perish, but have everlasting life”.

There had been one reaching out to me (that I had ignored) as I walked through my life. Oh Lord, I thank you for rescuing me from the father of lies, from my arrogant and prideful heart.

Thank You Lord for pursuing me, for Loving me, for Patience and Grace.

There is still time. If you are ready to turn to Jesus Christ, ask Solomon, “How can I be saved?”

Why Jesus Christ Matters

Most people do not begin the day thinking about eternity. Life is filled with work, bills, family, and the problems directly in front of us. Yet the deepest questions do not disappear. Why am I here? Can God forgive me? What happens when this life ends? Jesus Christ matters because He stands at the center of every one of those questions—and because He did not come merely to teach us, but to save us.

Questions Worth Asking

What Is the Gospel? Why Does God Allow Suffering? Is the Bible Trustworthy?

Why Jesus Christ Matters

Most people do not begin the day thinking about eternity. They wake up, go to work, pay bills, care for family, solve problems, and try to make it through another week. Life keeps moving, and the deepest questions are easy to postpone.

But they do not go away.

Why am I here? What has gone wrong with the world? What has gone wrong inside me? Is there a God who knows me? Can I be forgiven? What happens when this life ends?

Jesus Christ matters because He stands at the center of every one of those questions.

The Bible does not present Jesus merely as a wise teacher, a religious reformer, or a good man whose example we should admire. It declares that He is the eternal Son of God who entered the world He created.

“All things were made through Him, and without Him nothing was made that was made.”

John 1:3, NKJV

The One who walked the dusty roads of Galilee is the One through whom the stars were formed. He knew hunger, weariness, sorrow, rejection, and pain, yet He never ceased to be Lord. Jesus matters because God did not remain distant from our suffering. He came near.

And He came for a reason.

Our greatest problem is not simply that life is difficult or that the world is unfair. Our deepest problem is sin. We have turned from God, chosen our own way, and failed to love Him and one another as we should. Scripture says plainly:

“For all have sinned and fall short of the glory of God.”

Romans 3:23, NKJV

That includes respectable people and ruined people, religious people and skeptical people, the outwardly successful and the openly broken. We may hide our guilt from neighbors, family, and even ourselves, but we cannot hide it from God.

Yet Jesus did not come merely to expose our sin. He came to save us from it.

He lived the righteous life we have not lived. He showed mercy to the ashamed, touched those others avoided, welcomed repentant sinners, confronted proud hearts, and spoke truth without fear. Then He willingly went to the cross.

There, Jesus took upon Himself the judgment sinners deserved.

“Christ died for our sins according to the Scriptures.”

1 Corinthians 15:3, NKJV

The cross was not an unfortunate ending to an otherwise inspiring life. It was the mission. Jesus stood in the place of guilty people. The sinless Son of God bore sin so that sinners could be forgiven, cleansed, and reconciled to God.

But the cross was not the end.

On the third day, Jesus rose bodily from the grave. Death could not hold Him. The tomb was opened, the stone was rolled away, and the risen Christ appeared to His followers. His resurrection was God’s declaration that the sacrifice had been accepted and that death itself had been defeated.

Jesus matters because He is alive.

He is not merely a memory from ancient history. He reigns now. He hears prayer now. He forgives now. He changes lives now. He gives peace to the guilty, hope to the grieving, strength to the weary, and eternal life to those who trust Him.

And this is where the question becomes personal.

It is possible to know facts about Jesus and still refuse Him. It is possible to admire His teachings while keeping Him at a safe distance. It is possible to attend church, own a Bible, and speak religious language without ever surrendering to Christ.

Jesus did not say, “Consider Me when convenient.” He said:

“I am the way, the truth, and the life. No one comes to the Father except through Me.”

John 14:6, NKJV

That claim leaves no room for treating Him as one spiritual option among many. If Jesus is who He said He is, then no decision matters more than what you do with Him.

Your achievements cannot erase your sin. Your good intentions cannot conquer death. Religion cannot manufacture eternal life. Money cannot purchase peace with God. Time will not make the question disappear.

But Jesus can save you.

He invites you to turn from sin, stop trusting in yourself, and place your faith in Him. Salvation is not earned by becoming good enough. It is received by grace through faith in the crucified and risen Christ.

“Believe on the Lord Jesus Christ, and you will be saved.”

Acts 16:31, NKJV

Jesus matters because without Him we remain separated from God, guilty in our sin, and unprepared for eternity.

Jesus matters because He loved sinners enough to die in their place.

Jesus matters because He rose from the dead.

Jesus matters because He is Lord.

And Jesus matters because, right now, He is calling you to come.

Do not wait for a better time, a cleaner past, or a more convenient moment. Come to Christ as you are. Ask Solomon 2.0, “How can I be saved?”

Who Is Jesus Christ?

Most people have heard the name Jesus.

Some know Him as the child in the manger. Others think of Him as a great teacher, a miracle worker, or a moral example. Some respect Him while keeping Him at a distance. Others have rejected a version of Jesus they were never properly taught in the first place.

But the question remains:

Who is Jesus Christ?

The Bible’s answer is far greater than many expect.

Jesus did not begin His existence in Bethlehem. Before He was born into this world, He already was. He is the eternal Son of God.

The Gospel of John begins with these words:

“In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God.”

John 1:1, NKJV

A few verses later, John makes clear that “the Word” is Jesus Christ:

“And the Word became flesh and dwelt among us.”

John 1:14, NKJV

Jesus is not a created being who later became important. He is not an angel elevated above the others. He is not merely a prophet sent to speak about God.

He is God the Son.

He existed before the world began. He shared glory with the Father before creation. All things were made through Him and for Him.

“For by Him all things were created that are in heaven and that are on earth, visible and invisible.”

Colossians 1:16, NKJV

The hands that touched the sick were the hands through which the universe was made. The voice that spoke peace to frightened disciples was the voice that commanded light to shine in the darkness.

Yet the eternal Son of God willingly entered His creation.

Jesus was conceived by the Holy Spirit and born of the virgin Mary. He became truly human without ceasing to be truly God.

He grew. He learned. He became tired. He felt hunger and thirst. He wept at the grave of a friend. He experienced rejection, grief, pain, and temptation.

But He never sinned.

“For we do not have a High Priest who cannot sympathize with our weaknesses, but was in all points tempted as we are, yet without sin.”

Hebrews 4:15, NKJV

Jesus is fully God and fully man.

That truth is essential. If He were only a man, He could not save the world. If He had not truly become human, He could not stand in our place.

But because He is both God and man, He is the perfect Mediator between a holy God and sinful humanity.

Jesus did more than teach good principles.

He spoke with an authority unlike anyone around Him. He did not merely repeat the words of earlier teachers. He declared the truth of God with His own authority.

He forgave sins.

When Jesus told a paralyzed man that his sins were forgiven, the religious leaders understood the seriousness of His words. They asked:

“Who can forgive sins but God alone?”

Mark 2:7, NKJV

Their question was correct.

Only God can ultimately forgive sin. Jesus was not stealing God’s authority. He was revealing His identity.

He commanded storms, and the wind obeyed Him.

He cast out demons.

He healed the blind, cleansed lepers, strengthened crippled bodies, and raised the dead.

These miracles were not performances meant to entertain a crowd. They were signs. They revealed His compassion, His authority, and His identity.

Jesus also made claims no ordinary teacher could honestly make.

“He who has seen Me has seen the Father.”

John 14:9, NKJV

“Before Abraham was, I AM.”

John 8:58, NKJV

“I and My Father are one.”

John 10:30, NKJV

Those who heard Him understood what He was claiming. Jesus was not merely saying that He agreed with God. He was claiming a unique oneness with the Father and using language that belonged to God Himself.

This is why Jesus cannot be reduced to a nice religious teacher.

Jesus must be received as He truly is: the eternal Son, the promised Messiah, the Lord.

But Jesus did not come merely to prove who He was.

He came to save.

The Son of God entered a fallen world knowing where His path would lead. He knew He would be rejected. He knew He would be betrayed, beaten, mocked, and crucified.

Still, He came.

At the cross, Jesus willingly gave His life for sinners.

“For the Son of Man has come to seek and to save that which was lost.”

Luke 19:10, NKJV

Jesus was not helpless before His enemies. He said plainly:

“No one takes it from Me, but I lay it down of Myself.”

John 10:18, NKJV

He gave Himself.

On the cross, the sinless Christ bore the judgment of sin. He stood in the place of guilty people. He paid a debt He did not owe for people who could never pay it themselves.

“Who Himself bore our sins in His own body on the tree.”

1 Peter 2:24, NKJV

But Jesus did not remain in the grave.

On the third day, He rose bodily from the dead.

Christian faith does not rest upon a teacher whose life ended in defeat. It rests upon the risen Lord.

The resurrection declared that death had been conquered, Christ’s sacrifice had been accepted, and His claims were true.

Jesus appeared to His disciples. He spoke with them. He ate with them. They touched Him. He was not a vision, memory, or symbol.

He was alive.

Afterward, He ascended into heaven, where He reigns at the right hand of the Father.

Jesus is not waiting to become King someday.

He is King now.

He has been given all authority in heaven and on earth. He intercedes for His people. He builds His Church. He rules over history, even when the world appears chaotic and evil seems powerful.

“Jesus Christ is the same yesterday, today, and forever.”

Hebrews 13:8, NKJV

And He will return.

The Jesus who came first in humility will come again in glory. He will not return as a helpless infant or a suffering servant. He will return as Judge and King.

Every wrong will be answered. Every hidden thing will be brought to light. Death will not have the final word. Evil will not rule forever.

Scripture says:

“Behold, He is coming with clouds, and every eye will see Him.”

Revelation 1:7, NKJV

So who is Jesus Christ?

He is the eternal Son of God.

He is the Creator who entered His creation.

He is fully God and fully man.

He is the sinless Savior.

He is the Lamb who died for sinners.

He is the risen Lord.

He is the reigning King.

He is the coming Judge.

And He is the only way to the Father.

The question is not merely whether Jesus once lived. The question is whether you will receive Him for who He is.

You may admire Him and still remain far from Him. You may know Bible stories and still refuse His rule. You may speak His name without trusting His sacrifice.

Jesus does not call you merely to study Him.

He calls you to follow Him.

He calls you to repent, believe, and surrender.

Thomas once stood before the risen Christ and said:

“My Lord and my God!”

John 20:28, NKJV

That is the right response.

Jesus Christ is not merely part of Christianity.

Jesus Christ is Christianity.

Without Him, there is no Gospel, no forgiveness, no reconciliation with God, and no eternal hope.

But with Him, sinners are forgiven, the lost are found, the guilty are cleansed, and the dead are promised resurrection.

Jesus Christ is Lord.

Ask Solomon 2.0, “How can I know Jesus Christ for myself?”

Can God Forgive Me?

Perhaps you are not asking this question because you made a few ordinary mistakes.

Perhaps you have hurt people physically, morally, or emotionally.

Perhaps you have betrayed someone who trusted you, destroyed a marriage, abandoned your children, stolen from others, lived dishonestly, abused your authority, surrendered yourself to addiction, or carried bitterness and hatred for years.

Perhaps there are things in your past that you have never told another living soul.

Maybe your thoughts sound something like this:

“Can God forgive even such a heathen as me?”

Or perhaps your thoughts are even darker:

“You do not know what I have done. Charles Manson has nothing on me. Why should I expect forgiveness of any kind?”

If that is where you are, listen carefully.

The Bible does not answer your question by pretending your sins are small.

Some sins leave scars that never completely disappear in this life. Some destroy families. Some leave lifelong emotional wounds. Some result in imprisonment. Some bring broken health, terrible mental anguish, shattered reputations, financial ruin, or consequences that continue long after genuine repentance begins.

God never calls evil good.

He never asks us to pretend that sin does not matter.

The cross itself proves how serious sin really is.

Yet the Bible also declares something astonishing.

No one who truly comes to God through Jesus Christ in repentance and faith is beyond His mercy.

Jesus Christ came into the world to save sinners.

Not respectable sinners.

Not merely people with a few regrets.

Sinners.

The Apostle Paul had persecuted Christians before Christ saved him. Looking back upon his former life, he wrote:

“Christ Jesus came into the world to save sinners, of whom I am chief.”

1 Timothy 1:15, NKJV

King David committed adultery and arranged the death of an innocent man. When confronted by God, he confessed his sin and cried out for mercy. God forgave him, although David still lived with painful earthly consequences.

The criminal hanging beside Jesus had no opportunity to repair his past or prove himself worthy. He simply turned to Christ in humble faith.

Jesus answered him:

“Assuredly, I say to you, today you will be with Me in Paradise.”

Luke 23:43, NKJV

God’s forgiveness does not always remove earthly consequences.

A forgiven thief may still serve a prison sentence.

A forgiven drunk driver may still live with debilitating injuries or the knowledge that another person was permanently harmed.

A forgiven husband or wife may spend years rebuilding trust—or may never fully recover what was destroyed.

A forgiven criminal may still stand before an earthly judge and accept lawful punishment.

A forgiven addict may still face severe physical, emotional, or mental consequences while learning to walk in obedience to Christ.

Repentance often requires confession, restitution where possible, accepting responsibility, seeking treatment or counseling, protecting those who were harmed, and accepting the lawful consequences of our actions.

The Gospel is not an escape from justice.

It is God’s provision of mercy in the midst of justice.

Some earthly payments may be terrible.

They may include prison, destroyed relationships, chronic illness, disabling injuries, unbearable memories, or consequences that last for the remainder of this earthly life.

But God is above every prison wall, every diagnosis, every memory, and every consequence.

None of those things place you beyond the reach of His grace.

God is not surprised by your past.

He knew every sin you would ever commit before you committed it.

He is not shocked when you finally confess the truth.

He is not disgusted by the sinner who comes before Him in honest submission, with a broken and repentant heart.

Jesus Christ is never unwilling to receive the person who truly comes to Him in repentance and faith.

Jesus said:

“The one who comes to Me I will by no means cast out.”

John 6:37, NKJV

That promise still stands.

You do not have to clean yourself up before coming to Christ.

You come because you cannot clean yourself up.

You come because you need a Savior.

True repentance is more than feeling guilty. It is agreeing with God about your sin, turning away from it, and placing your complete trust in Jesus Christ—His death on the cross, His burial, and His bodily resurrection—as your only hope of forgiveness and eternal life.

You may carry memories that haunt you.

You may still have terrible consequences to face.

You may need to confess, apologize, make restitution, seek help, surrender destructive habits, protect others from further harm, or accept lawful punishment.

But none of those things prevent God from doing what only He can do.

He can forgive.

He can cleanse.

He can adopt you as His child.

He can give you a new heart and a new life through Jesus Christ.

The greatness of your sin is never greater than the greatness of the Savior who died and rose again.

If your heart is being stirred today, do not walk away.

Come honestly.

Come humbly.

Come without excuses.

Come to Jesus Christ.

There is still time.

Ask Solomon 2.0, “How can I be saved?”
